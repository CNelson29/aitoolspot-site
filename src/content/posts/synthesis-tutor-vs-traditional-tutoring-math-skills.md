## What is Synthesis?

Synthesis is an AI-powered math learning platform originally built as a school for SpaceX employees' children. CEO Josh Dahn and his team opened it to the public in 2022, and it has since grown to over 500,000 active students. The premise is simple: replace rote drills and passive instruction with adaptive, game-based problem-solving that feels more like Minecraft than homework.

The core product — **Synthesis Tutor** — covers arithmetic through pre-algebra (roughly grades K–8) and uses a mastery-based progression engine. Every session is 20–30 minutes. The AI watches how a child solves problems, identifies conceptual gaps in real time, and adjusts difficulty instantly — something a human tutor typically needs several sessions to calibrate.

## Key Features

- **Adaptive AI Engine:** Continuously adjusts problem difficulty and topic sequencing based on performance, not grade level. A 2nd grader doing 5th-grade multiplication will get it.
- **Game-Based Missions:** Problems are wrapped in narrative missions, building intrinsic motivation. Kids often ask to play Synthesis — a rare feat for math software.
- **Mastery Gates:** Students cannot skip ahead until they demonstrate true understanding, preventing the gaps that plague traditional tutoring.

- **No Teacher Required:** The platform is self-directed. Most kids ages 6+ can use it independently after a brief onboarding session.
- **Multi-Device:** Browser-based on desktop, iPad-optimized on tablet. No app install required.

## Pros and Cons

### Pros

- Dramatically more affordable than in-person or online human tutoring
- Daily practice possible — not limited to 1–2 sessions per week
- AI adapts faster and more precisely than most human tutors can
- Kids genuinely enjoy it — engagement is the platform's biggest differentiator
- Transparent progress data for parents
- Generous 30-day free trial removes purchase risk

### Cons

- No human emotional connection — some children need encouragement from a real person
- Scope is limited to K–8 math; no high school algebra II, geometry proofs, or calculus
- Requires self-discipline from the child; parents need to build the habit initially
- Not designed for standardized test prep (SAT, ACT, AMC)
- Occasional UI inconsistencies reported on older Android tablets

## Who Should NOT Use Synthesis

- **High schoolers:** The curriculum tops out around pre-algebra. Students in Algebra II or beyond need a different tool.
- **Kids with diagnosed learning disabilities:** Students with dyscalculia or who require an IEP-based approach need a specialist, not an AI platform.
- **Students needing test-specific prep:** If the goal is SAT Math or AMC 8/10, look at dedicated prep courses instead.
- **Children who thrive on human mentorship:** Some kids are unmotivated by screens regardless of gamification. If your child already has a tutor they love, don't fix what isn't broken.
- **Families wanting curriculum alignment:** Synthesis uses its own mastery sequence, not Common Core grade-band pacing, which can confuse parents comparing to school grades.

## FAQ

### Is Synthesis better than a human math tutor?

For daily practice and adaptive pacing in K–8 math, yes — Synthesis is more consistent and far cheaper. For emotional support, complex problem explanation, or high school math, a human tutor is still superior.

### What age is Synthesis designed for?

The sweet spot is ages 5–14 (kindergarten through pre-algebra). Older students will find the curriculum too limited.

### Does Synthesis replace school math entirely?

No. It supplements school or homeschool curriculum. It accelerates mastery but does not replace structured instruction in new concepts.

### How long does a typical Synthesis session take?

Sessions are designed to be 20–30 minutes. The AI ends a mission naturally at that point, making it easy to build a daily habit without burnout.

### Is there a free trial?

Yes — 30 days free, no credit card required. It is one of the most generous trials in the ed-tech space.

### Can multiple children share one account?

Family plans support up to 4 children under a single subscription, each with their own independent progress profile.
