## FAQ

### Is Notion AI better than Obsidian for knowledge management in 2026?

It depends on your needs. Notion AI is better for teams, collaboration, and AI-native features. Obsidian is better for privacy, local storage, and deep personal knowledge graphs. Neither is universally superior — they serve different knowledge management philosophies.

### Can Obsidian match Notion AI's artificial intelligence features?

Obsidian can approximate Notion AI's capabilities through community plugins like Smart Connections, Copilot, and Text Generator, but these require manual setup, API keys, and ongoing maintenance. Notion AI's integration is seamless and requires zero configuration, giving it a significant usability advantage.

### Does Notion AI work offline?

Notion has limited offline functionality for viewing cached pages, but Notion AI requires an active internet connection to function. Obsidian, by contrast, is fully offline-capable with local AI plugins that run models on-device.

### How much does Notion AI cost in 2026?

Notion AI is available as a $10/user/month add-on to any Notion plan, or bundled in the Plus plan at $18/user/month and Business plan at $25/user/month. Enterprise pricing is custom. Obsidian is free for personal use with optional paid Sync ($96/year) and Publish ($192/year) services.

### Is my data safe with Notion AI?

Notion uses enterprise-grade encryption and complies with GDPR, SOC 2 Type II, and other standards. However, your data is stored on Notion's cloud servers. If absolute data sovereignty is required, Obsidian's local-first model is the safer choice.

### Can I use both Notion AI and Obsidian together?

Yes, and many knowledge workers in 2026 do exactly this. A common workflow is using Notion AI for team wikis, project management, and collaborative documents, while using Obsidian for personal research, journaling, and deep knowledge linking. The two tools complement each other well.

### What is the biggest advantage of Notion AI over Obsidian?

The biggest advantage is the native, zero-configuration AI that works across your entire workspace — including databases, pages, and external connectors. The ability to ask natural language questions and get cited answers from your knowledge base, combined with AI database autofill, is a capability Obsidian cannot match without significant plugin setup.
