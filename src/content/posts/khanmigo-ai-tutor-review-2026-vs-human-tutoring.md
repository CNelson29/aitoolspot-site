## FAQ

### Is Khanmigo better than a human tutor in 2026?

For cost and availability, yes — Khanmigo wins decisively. For emotional support, advanced subjects, and accountability, human tutors still have an edge. Most families benefit from using both: Khanmigo for daily practice and a human tutor for periodic deep-dive sessions.

### What age is Khanmigo appropriate for?

Khanmigo is designed for students roughly ages 8 and up (grades 3+). Younger children can use it with parental guidance, but the text-based interface works best once students have solid reading skills.

### Does Khanmigo give students the answers?

No — this is one of its defining features. Khanmigo uses the Socratic method, asking guiding questions rather than providing direct answers. This promotes genuine understanding and discourages academic dishonesty.

### Can teachers use Khanmigo for free?

Yes. Verified educators get free access to Khanmigo's teacher tools, including lesson plan generation, quiz creation, and student progress dashboards. This is part of Khan Academy's nonprofit mission.

### How does Khanmigo handle incorrect information?

Like all AI systems, Khanmigo can occasionally produce errors (hallucinations). Khan Academy has implemented fact-checking layers and curriculum alignment to minimize this, but parents and students should verify critical information, especially in science and history.

### Is Khanmigo safe for children?

Yes. Khanmigo has robust content filtering, stays strictly on educational topics, and is designed with child safety as a core principle. It will not engage with off-topic, harmful, or inappropriate content.

### What subjects does Khanmigo cover in 2026?

Khanmigo covers math (K–12 through pre-calculus and statistics), science, history, English/writing, SAT/ACT prep, AP courses, computer science, and creative writing. Spanish, French, and Hindi language support is available, with more languages in beta.
