## What is Synthesis?

Now available to families worldwide, it uses adaptive algorithms and game-based challenges to teach children mathematics in a way that feels engaging rather than tedious. Unlike traditional tutoring apps that rely on repetitive drills, Synthesis builds genuine problem-solving skills through collaborative puzzles, simulations, and real-world scenarios.

The platform targets children aged 6–14 and positions itself as a supplement — not a replacement — for school-based math education. Its core philosophy is that kids learn best when they are genuinely challenged and intrinsically motivated, rather than rewarded with hollow gold stars.

## FAQ

### Is Synthesis suitable for all grade levels?

Synthesis is best suited for children aged 6–14, roughly covering grades 1 through 8. Content becomes richer and more challenging as children progress, but it is not designed for high school advanced math topics like calculus or trigonometry.

### Does Synthesis replace school math?

No. Synthesis is explicitly designed as a supplement to school-based math education. It builds problem-solving skills and mathematical thinking but does not follow a rigid sequential curriculum the way school programs do.

### How much time should my child spend on Synthesis each week?

Synthesis recommends 2–3 sessions per week, each lasting 20–30 minutes. Consistency matters more than duration — short, regular sessions produce better results than occasional marathon sessions.

### Is there a free version of Synthesis?

Synthesis offers a 14-day free trial with full access to all features. After the trial, a paid subscription is required. There is no permanently free tier.

### How does Synthesis compare to Khan Academy?

Khan Academy is free and covers a broader range of subjects with a more traditional instructional approach. Synthesis is paid, math-focused, and uses game-based adaptive learning for deeper engagement. They serve different needs and can be used together effectively.

### Can multiple children in one family use the same account?

Each child requires their own account so the AI can track individual progress accurately. Synthesis offers family pricing discounts for households with multiple children.
