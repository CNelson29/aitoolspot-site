## What is Grammarly?

Grammarly is one of the most recognized AI-powered writing assistants on the market. Launched over a decade ago, it has evolved from a simple grammar checker into a full-scale writing intelligence platform. In 2026, Grammarly continues to serve millions of users — from students and bloggers to enterprise teams — helping them write clearly, correctly, and confidently across virtually every platform they use.

At its core, Grammarly analyzes your text in real time, flagging grammar errors, spelling mistakes, punctuation issues, and stylistic weaknesses. But in 2026, it goes far beyond that. With its integrated **GrammarlyGO** generative AI engine, users can now draft content, rewrite sentences, adjust tone, and generate ideas — all within the same interface. Whether you're writing an email, a LinkedIn post, a college essay, or a business report, Grammarly positions itself as your always-on writing coach.

It works as a browser extension, a desktop app, a Microsoft Word add-in, and a mobile keyboard — making it one of the most versatile writing tools available today.

## Key Features
- **Real-Time Grammar and Spell Check:** Grammarly's foundational feature remains best-in-class. It catches errors that standard spell checkers miss, including context-sensitive mistakes like confusing 'affect' and 'effect.'- **Tone Detection:** Grammarly analyzes the emotional tone of your writing and suggests adjustments based on your intended audience — whether you want to sound confident, friendly, or formal.- **GrammarlyGO (Generative AI):** The standout upgrade in recent years. GrammarlyGO lets you generate full drafts, rewrite paragraphs, summarize content, and brainstorm ideas using AI prompts. It's deeply integrated and context-aware.- **Plagiarism Checker:** Available on Premium and Business plans, this feature scans your text against billions of web pages to ensure originality — essential for students and content creators.- **Style and Clarity Suggestions:** Beyond grammar, Grammarly flags wordy sentences, passive voice overuse, and unclear phrasing, pushing your writing toward greater impact.- **Goals and Audience Settings:** You can set your writing goals — audience type, formality level, domain, and intent — so Grammarly tailors its suggestions specifically to your context.- **Cross-Platform Integration:** Works seamlessly in Chrome, Firefox, Safari, Edge, Google Docs, Microsoft Word, Outlook, Gmail, Slack, and more.- **Analytics Dashboard (Business):** Teams get access to writing analytics, showing communication trends and areas for improvement across the organization.- **Brand Tones (Business):** Enterprise users can define custom brand voice guidelines that Grammarly enforces across all team communications.

## Who Should NOT Use Grammarly

Despite its strengths, Grammarly isn't the right fit for everyone. Here's who might want to look elsewhere:
- **Privacy-sensitive professionals:** Lawyers, doctors, and executives handling confidential information should be cautious. Grammarly's cloud-based processing means your text passes through external servers. For highly sensitive documents, a local tool or manual proofreading may be safer.- **Writers with a strong, unconventional voice:** If your writing style intentionally breaks grammar rules — think literary fiction, experimental prose, or dialect-heavy content — Grammarly's suggestions can feel intrusive and may dilute your voice.- **Budget-conscious users who need advanced features:** If you need the plagiarism checker and AI writing tools but can't justify the Premium price, alternatives like ProWritingAid offer comparable features at a lower annual cost.- **Non-English writers:** Grammarly is primarily optimized for English. While it supports some other languages, its accuracy and feature depth drop significantly outside of English content.- **Offline workers:** If you frequently write in environments without reliable internet access, Grammarly's cloud dependency makes it impractical.

## FAQ

### Is Grammarly free to use in 2026?

Yes, Grammarly offers a free plan that covers basic grammar and spelling corrections. However, advanced features like tone detection, the plagiarism checker, and GrammarlyGO require a Premium or Business subscription.

### How much does Grammarly Premium cost in 2026?

Grammarly Premium costs approximately $12 per month when billed annually. Monthly billing is available at a higher rate. Pricing may vary slightly by region and promotional periods.

### Is Grammarly safe to use for confidential documents?

Grammarly processes text through its cloud servers, which raises privacy considerations for highly sensitive content. For confidential legal, medical, or business documents, review Grammarly's privacy policy carefully or consider alternative offline tools.

### Does Grammarly work with Google Docs?

Yes. Grammarly integrates directly with Google Docs via its browser extension, providing real-time suggestions as you write. It also works with Microsoft Word, Gmail, Outlook, Slack, and most web-based text editors.

### How does Grammarly compare to ProWritingAid in 2026?

Both are strong tools. Grammarly excels in real-time suggestions, platform integrations, and ease of use. ProWritingAid offers deeper long-form writing reports and is often cheaper annually. Grammarly is better for everyday writing across multiple platforms; ProWritingAid suits authors and long-form content creators.

### What is GrammarlyGO?

GrammarlyGO is Grammarly's integrated generative AI feature. It allows users to generate drafts, rewrite text, adjust tone, summarize content, and brainstorm ideas using AI prompts — all within the Grammarly interface. It's available on Premium and Business plans with monthly usage limits.

### Can Grammarly detect AI-generated content?

As of 2026, Grammarly does not offer a dedicated AI content detection feature for end users. Its plagiarism checker focuses on matching text against existing web content rather than identifying AI authorship.
